# Rosso — Governed, Zero-Trust Autonomy for AI Agents

**Rosso is a research project that ships.** We are chasing questions no one has settled — what an agent's *identity* means when it rewrites its own behavior at runtime, how to lend authority to something non-deterministic and keep it bounded — and we build a production platform because those ideas only earn trust when they run.

Software is learning to act on its own. AI agents no longer just answer questions — they plan, call tools, spend money, move data, and delegate to other agents over hours or days. The unit of computation is no longer a request that returns; it is an autonomous actor that reasons, remembers, and reaches into the world on someone's behalf. Our infrastructure was not built for this: agents are autonomous, privileged, and steered by untrusted input all at once, and the controls that made cloud-native software safe simply don't hold.

The industry is standardizing how agents *talk* — A2A between agents, MCP to tools. **Rosso builds the trust, memory, and governance fabric underneath them.** Where a strong standard exists — SPIFFE for workload identity, RFC 8693 for token exchange — we build on it and say so. Our contribution is what those standards do not yet reach, organized into three pillars.

**The Identity Fabric — who an agent is, and what it's made of.** Every agent gets a cryptographic identity rooted in the running workload, not a shared secret. Our frontier bet goes further than any standard today: we fold a signature-verified *skill digest* into that identity, so **a tampered or unknown skill changes who the agent is** — and the platform refuses to authorize it, fail-closed, before any tool call. Supply-chain integrity stops being an after-the-fact scan and becomes part of the agent's identity itself. Delegation, likewise, is driven toward per-intent, self-expiring authority that leaves no standing credential behind.

**The Governance Plane — what an agent may do, decided from full context.** A single, auditable decision point renders every verdict from complete information — caller, callee, user, task, budget, and data lineage — and our research aims for policy the platform *derives from human-language intent and continuously audits itself against*. A human stays in the loop for what matters, evolving from mobile approvals today toward a **learned surrogate agent** that models one user's judgment and stands in for them on the routine, reserving people for the genuinely high-risk. Cost becomes a term in the authorization decision, not just a bill.

**The Runtime Substrate — how an agent lives, remembers, is fed, and recovers.** Session state is externalized into a durable log, so agents survive eviction, recover with zero lost work, scale to zero when idle, and *move* between runtimes — recovery built on proven event-sourcing, with one twist: the crash-recovery checkpoint *is* the agent's own context-compaction summary, not an opaque blob. What enters the model's window is itself governed — context assembled and pruned by the platform, not the agent, with each fragment's **provenance feeding the authorization decision** so lineage can gate a response before the model acts. Every credential lives only in an identity-aware egress broker, so **the agent uses keys it can never read**. And as a longer-range research bet, we are exploring a memory layer that reorganizes itself under live query pressure — tracking how an enterprise's language shifts without ever retraining the model.

**The through-line: the platform absorbs the hard problems so agents don't have to.** The innovation is not any single box — it's the insistence that identity, integrity, delegation, policy, cost, context, memory, and reach compose into *one enforceable path*, and the willingness to invent the missing pieces rather than stop at what the standards already give us.

Rosso is our contribution, built in the open on A2A, MCP, and SPIFFE: the platform primitives for governed, zero-trust agent autonomy.
